About Myself

On December 25th, 1999, I left the USA for a three-month, post-graduation trip to Southeast Asia. The idea was to backpack around Thailand, Cambodia and Vietnam and then return home and start a career in the sports industry (I wanted to be a Sports Agent).

When I landed in Bangkok, I had $1500 USD to my name and that was it. So I booked a $1 per night dorm room bed inside of a crumbling old wooden guesthouse in the center of the city and let the adventure begin from there. The funny thing is, that adventure is still going on today. In the end, I never went home after those three months.

The Beauty Of Himalyas

The Himalayas in Nepal are a breathtaking range of mountains that form a natural border between Nepal and the Tibetan region of China. They are among the most dramatic and rugged landscapes on earth, featuring some of the highest peaks in the world, including Mount Everest (Sagarmatha in Nepali), which rises to a staggering 8,848 meters above sea level. The Himalayas stretch across the northern part of Nepal, covering nearly 75% of the countryâ€™s total area, and play a crucial role in the cultural, spiritual, and economic life of the Nepalese people.

The Himalayas are not just a geographical marvel but also a cultural and spiritual hub. They are home to various ethnic groups, including the Sherpas, Gurungs, and Thakalis, each with their distinct language, traditions, and customs. The Sherpas, in particular, are famous for their mountaineering skills and their deep spiritual connection to the mountains.
